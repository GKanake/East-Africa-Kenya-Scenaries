Mount Kenya

At 5,199 meters, Mount Kenya is the highest mountain in Kenya and the second highest in Africa.

It is an extinct stratovolcano with multiple peaks, offering breathtaking scenery and rich biodiversity.

Mount Elgon

Mount Elgon (4,321 m) is an extinct shield volcano, shared between Kenya and Uganda.

It features vast calderas, numerous caves, and rich flora and fauna.

Aberdare Ranges

The Aberdare Range, with Satima Peak reaching 4,001 meters, is a beautiful mountain range in central Kenya.

The range is known for its scenic landscapes and diverse wildlife.

Mount Meru

Mount Meru (4,562 m) is located in Tanzania, near the Kenyan border.

It is an active stratovolcano, offering stunning hiking experiences.

Ol Donyo Sabuk

Ol Donyo Sabuk, also known as Kilimambogo, is a solitary mountain rising to 2,145 meters in central Kenya.

Located northeast of Nairobi, it is part of Ol Donyo Sabuk National Park, a haven for wildlife and hiking enthusiasts.

The mountain offers panoramic views of the surrounding plains and is historically significant due to Lord William Northrup McMillan’s residence at its base.

Mount Kilimanjaro

Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa’s highest peak at 5,895 meters, is a world-renowned stratovolcano located in Tanzania, near the Kenyan border.

Though primarily in Tanzania, the mountain is visible from Kenya and plays a crucial role in the Amboseli ecosystem.

Its snow-capped peaks and diverse climatic zones make it a popular trekking destination for climbers from around the world.

Lake Naivasha

Lake Naivasha is a freshwater lake in Kenya, located northwest of Nairobi, in the Great Rift Valley.

The lake is famous for its rich biodiversity, supporting a variety of bird species, including fish eagles, pelicans, and flamingos.

It is also home to hippos and serves as an important water source for local communities and wildlife.

Mount Longonot

Mount Longonot (2,776 m) is a stratovolcano located in the Great Rift Valley.

It features a crater at the summit and offers stunning panoramic views.
